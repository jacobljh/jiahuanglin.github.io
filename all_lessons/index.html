Knowledge Distillation

A small model can learn more from a big model's answer than from the right answer. Two condensed lessons on three knobs — what the student matches, how the gap is measured, where the data comes from. Lesson 01: foundations — dark knowledge, the soft-target loss with the temperature/T² gradient correction derived from scratch, four honest lenses on why it works (and the capacity-gap paradox), and the WHAT knob (response, feature & relational, born-again). Lesson 02: distilling generative LLMs — word- vs sequence-level KD and exposure bias, forward-vs-reverse-KL (mode-covering vs mode-seeking), on-policy distillation (GKD, MiniLLM) as RL with the teacher as a dense reward, how it composes with quantization & pruning, and the frontier (reasoning-trace / R1 distillation, black-box style-vs-substance, weak-to-strong).
