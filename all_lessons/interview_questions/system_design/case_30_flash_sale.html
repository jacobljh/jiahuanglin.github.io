Design a flash sale system

A flash sale is a controlled overload event. The goal is not to make every request succeed; it is to fail most requests cheaply and fairly.

0. Interview hinge

The hinge is the moment where the prompt stops being generic and the architecture becomes forced. For this case, that hinge is: Queue gating, stock counters, fairness, cache pre-warm, overload control.

1. Clarify the contract

The prompt is best treated as a product contract before it is treated as a component diagram. The system needs to do the following:

  • Handle extreme burst traffic at sale start.
  • Prevent overselling limited stock.
  • Keep product pages and checkout available.
  • Provide fair-ish admission.
  • Stop bots and repeated attempts where possible.

2. Put numbers on the shape

The exact numbers depend on the interviewer, but these are the estimates that should steer the architecture:

  • Peak QPS can be thousands of times normal traffic.
  • Only stock-count successful orders can exist; all other work is waste.
  • Database writes must be bounded by inventory, not by request volume.
  • Waiting room state can become a hot key if not partitioned.

5. Linearized design

Walk the design in the order a request or event actually moves. This keeps the interview grounded and exposes the first bottleneck naturally.

  1. 1. Pre-warm product, price, and sale metadata in CDN and cache.
  2. 2. Put users into a waiting room or admission queue before checkout.
  3. 3. Issue signed, short-lived admission tokens to a bounded number of users.
  4. 4. Reserve stock with atomic counters or serialized reservation workers.
  5. 5. Let admitted users complete checkout within a hold timeout.
  6. 6. Shed excess traffic at edge with clear sold-out or wait responses.

6. Deep dives

Gating

Letting everyone hit checkout creates database death. Gate before scarce resources. The queue protects the inventory service from the public internet.

Atomic stock

Inventory decrement must be conditional: decrement only if remaining stock is positive. Everything else can be eventually consistent.

Fairness

Perfect fairness is impossible under bot pressure and network variance. Define a policy: random lottery, first-come, or membership priority.

9. What to say in the interview

Strong signal
